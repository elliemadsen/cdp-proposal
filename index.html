project proposal

I propose to create a web-based interactive tool that overlays layers of graphical data onto urban geographies, enabling users to explore networks of meaning through spatial interfaces. By combining 3D city models, open geospatial data, and vector databases, the platform will render a navigable topography with multiple layers of data that each represent a distinct medium or way of knowing.

For example, the layers may include computer vision analyses of signage, hypertextual relationships among local websites, semantic mappings of neighborhoods, or infrastructural networks. Each layer is composed of a graph data structure that exposes connections between and across entities (e.g. neighborhoods, businesses, media, or urban features), revealing both spatial and semantic relationships.

graph topology

Small-world

Networks with tightly connected local clusters and short paths linking any two nodes, allowing fast communication and robustness.

Hierarchical

Networks structured in nested levels of organization, combining modular clusters with overarching control or flow from higher to lower levels.

HNSW:

hierarchical small world navigable graph

Small world networks are resilient.

The prevalence of small world networks in biological systems suggests this topology is evolutionarily advantaged against mutation or infection. When a node is deleted, network connectivity remains unchanged.

In urban systems, small-world topology improves fault tolerance to ensure that local disruptions do not cause systemic failures.

These network principles are a powerful framework for climate resiliency.
